## Concept summary
A liquidity raid is a move that takes resting liquidity around an obvious level. It can precede continuation or reversal; the raid itself does not decide direction.

## Evidence needed
After the raid, study reaction, spread, close, volume behavior, and structure. PipFury treats the raid as context that needs confirmation.

## Common mistake
Traders see a sweep and immediately fade the move. That is anticipation. A disciplined trader waits to see whether price rejects, accepts, or stalls.

## Invalidation
If price accepts beyond the swept level and continues with strength, the reversal thesis is invalidated.
