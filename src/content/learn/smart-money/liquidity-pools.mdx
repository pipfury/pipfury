## Definition
Liquidity pools are areas where resting orders may cluster, often around obvious highs, lows, equal highs, equal lows, and clean support or resistance.

## Why they matter
Markets often move toward liquidity because orders are needed for execution. This does not mean every sweep is manipulation or a reversal.

## How to study
Mark obvious levels first. Then observe whether price accepts beyond them or rejects after taking liquidity.

## Risk note
A liquidity pool is a location, not an entry.
