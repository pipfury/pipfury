## Definition
No supply describes weak selling pressure after a decline or into a test area. It can matter when the market has already shown signs of strength.

## Confirmation
A no-supply read is stronger when price refuses to continue lower and later shows demand response. It is weaker when price simply drifts without structure.

## What it is not
No supply is not a buy signal by itself. It is a context clue that must be tested against location, risk, and confirmation.

## Review question
Was selling actually absent, or was the market simply inactive?
